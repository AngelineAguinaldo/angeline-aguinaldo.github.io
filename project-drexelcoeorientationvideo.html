Drexel College of Engineering Orientation Video

This video showcases a typical ten weeks of a Drexel Engineering student. This video is showcased at the Drexel University College of Engineering and School of Biomedical Engineering Orientation Days.

Background

Drexel University's academic calender is divided into a quarter-based system (divided into Fall, Winter, Spring, and Summer quarters) to accommodate for the co-operative education sessions (6 months). Therefore, each quarter consists of ten academic weeks. Due the brevity of the quarters, the curriculums are adapted to be accelerated and highly rigourous. These conditions ultimately lead to silently understood routine among Drexel students as shown in the video.

This project was intended to be light-hearted and comedic. All the actors and crew involved are proud to be part of the Drexel University community.

Project Details

This was a medium-scale film project sponsored by the Drexel College of Engineering.

A few words

After my video was showcased at Drexel University's Introduce a Girl to Engineering day, I was approached by the Dean of Drexel University's College of Engineering asking if I would interested in producing a video for orientation days. Eagerly, I jumped on my first medium-scale film project as a third-year student. I was responsible for ideation, project planning, team recruitment, and production. I worked with an incredible team of Drexel students, most of whom are featured in the video. I am very thankful to have had to opportunity to play an integral role in welcoming our incoming freshman engineers.
